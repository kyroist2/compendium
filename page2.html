Of Ireland

I dreamed of a land far away
With cows, sheep, potatoes and hay
Sprawling green luscious hills
Vegetables and flour mills
An honest life, I planned to live
In Dublin, Galway, or Limerick
On my paper I drew this place
Beautiful farms, my fingers traced
But soon the teacher called me out
So no longer I could dream about
The country that led my mind astray
Of Ireland, I’ll dream some other day
